## Pre-Surgery (Prehab)

**Goals:** Achieve full knee extension, minimize pain and swelling, develop quad control, walk normally.

### Mobility

---

#### 🟢 Everyday Knee Extensions

**Description:** When resting, sitting or lying down, extend your surgical leg in front of you with the knee straight up. Allow gravity to gently continue to straighten the knee.  
**Dosage:** 20–30 minutes, 3–4 times per day.  
**Note:** Also perform supine or prone knee extensions before and after heel slides.

---

#### 🟢 Supine (on Back) Knee Extensions

**Description:** Lie on your back with your leg straight. Place a small towel roll under your heel — **not your knee** — and hold that position.  
**Dosage:** 20–30 minutes, 3–4 times per day.

---

#### 🔵 Prone (on Stomach) Knee Extensions

**Description:** Lie on your stomach with a small flat towel roll just above your knee on the thigh. Let your foot hang off the end of the bed or table.  
**Dosage:** 20–30 minutes, 3–4 times per day.

---

#### 🔵 Seated Passive-Assisted Knee Extensions

**Description:** Seated in a chair, place the foot of your non-operative leg under the heel of your surgical leg. Gently use your good leg to lift your surgical leg out as straight as possible while keeping it relaxed. Return to start.  
**Dosage:** 10–20 reps, 2–3 times per day.

---

#### 🟢 Heel Slides — Knee Flexion and Extension

**Description:** Lie on your back or sit. Bend the knee, keeping the foot in contact with the surface, then slide the foot to straighten the knee. Slight discomfort is acceptable; stop if there is pain.  
**Dosage:** 10–20 reps, 3–4 sets per day.

---

#### 🟢 Ankle Pumps

**Description:** Lie or sit with legs out straight and a towel roll under your ankle. Bring toes up and then back down, like pressing and releasing a gas pedal.  
**Dosage:** 15 reps every hour.

---

### Strength

---

#### 🟢 Quadriceps Set

**Description:** Lie on your back or sit with legs out straight. Tighten the top of your thigh, pushing your knee flat against the surface so the kneecap moves toward your hip. Focus purely on the quad — not glutes or hamstrings. Hold 5 seconds, then relax.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 3 times per day.

---

#### 🟢 Hamstring Set — on Your Back

**Description:** Lie on your back and bend your knee to bring your calf to 90 degrees. Push your heel down into the surface with mild to moderate pressure. Hold 6 seconds, then relax.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 1–3 times per day.

---

#### 🔵 Hamstring Set — on Your Stomach

**Description:** Lie on your stomach with a towel under the front lower part of your thigh. Bend your knee to bring your calf up to 90 degrees from the surface.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 1–3 times per day.

---

#### 🔵 Hamstring Set — Standing

**Description:** Stand on both feet with both crutches for support. Perform a gentle hamstring curl, bending your knee upward behind you. Hold 6 seconds, then relax.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 1–3 times per day.

---

#### 🟢 Hamstring / Quadriceps Co-Contraction

**Description:** Lie on your back with the operative leg straight. Push your heel down into the surface as if "digging in," then simultaneously tighten your quadriceps to try to straighten the leg. Push firmly but not enough to lift your body. Hold both sides of the thigh contracted for 5 seconds, then relax.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 3 times per day.

---

#### 🟢 Straight Leg Raises (avoiding quad lag)

**Description:** Lie on your back with the non-operative leg bent to 90°. Tighten the operative quad and hold it tight throughout. Lift the leg until your knee reaches the height of the other knee using a slow, controlled motion. Keep the knee as straight as possible to prevent "quad lag" (knee bending as you start to lift). Return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 3 times per day.  
**Prerequisite:** Begin after making progress with quadriceps sets and being able to do a straight leg raise.

---

#### 🟢 Prone Straight Leg Raises

**Description:** Lie on your stomach, legs shoulder-width apart. Tighten core and glutes. Lift only the operative leg off the surface, hold 2 seconds, then lower slowly. Do not rotate hips or arch your back.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 3 times per day.  
**Tip:** Place a pillow under your abdomen if you feel lower back pain.

---

#### 🔵 Standing Hip Extensions

**Description:** Stand tall with hands on a stable surface. Shift weight to the non-operative leg. With an upright trunk, lift the operative leg slightly behind you without rotating pelvis or trunk. Hold 2 seconds, return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps each leg, 3 times per day. Add resistance band as strength improves.

---

#### 🔵 Prone Hamstring Curls

**Description:** Lie on your stomach (towel roll under thigh for comfort). Bend your operative knee to move the heel up toward your back slowly and deliberately. Hold the bent position 5 seconds. Lower slowly over 2–3 seconds.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 3 times per day.

---

#### 🟢 Double-Leg Heel Raises (Calf Press)

**Description:** Stand with hands on a stable surface, feet shoulder-width apart, equal weight on both legs. Lift heels over a 2-second count, hold briefly, then lower slowly over 4 seconds. Move vertically — do not rock forward.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 1–3 times per day.

---

#### 🟡 Standing Hamstring Curls

**Description:** Stand with hands on stable surface, upright. Shift weight slightly to the uninjured leg. Bend the other knee to bring the heel up toward your back. Hold 5 seconds. Lower slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 1–3 times per day.  
**Prerequisite:** Comfortable with prone hamstring curls and able to bear weight on operative leg with control.

---

#### 🟢 Double-Leg Quarter Squats

**Description:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hands on waist. Bend slightly at hips, then at knees, lowering buttocks about 6 inches as if sitting in a chair. Keep weight evenly on both legs, trunk straight, and knees aligned over 2nd/3rd toes without going past your toes. Use a mirror to check weight distribution.  
**Dosage:** As directed, 3 times per day.

---

#### 🟢 Side-Lying Hip Abduction

**Description:** Lie on your non-operative side. Straighten the top (operative) leg, bend the bottom leg for comfort. Bring the top leg slightly behind your trunk with toes rotated in slightly. Lift the top leg using the hip (not back or side). Lower and repeat. Keep hips aligned — do not let trunk rotate back.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 1–3 times per day. Can place 2–3 pillows between knees at the start.

---

#### 🟡 Side-Lying Hip Adduction

**Description:** Lie on your side with operative leg down and extended straight. Pull the non-operative foot toward you and place it in front of your operative knee. Keep trunk and hips stable. Lift the operative leg off the surface, hold 2 seconds, lower slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 3 times per day.  
**Caution:** Only with surgeon/PT approval if you have had an MCL injury or repair.

## Phase 1 (Post-Surgery: Week 1 onward)

**Goals:** Achieve full knee extension, minimize pain and swelling, good quad control, walk normally.  
**New in Phase 1:** Standing Terminal Knee Extension, Single-Leg Heel Raises, Patellar Mobilization.

Most Phase 1 exercises are identical to Prehab. The exercises below are new additions in Phase 1.

---

### Mobility

Same exercises as Prehab: Everyday Knee Extensions, Supine/Prone Knee Extensions, Seated Passive-Assisted Extensions, Heel Slides, Ankle Pumps.

---

### Strength — New Exercises in Phase 1

---

#### 🔵 Standing Terminal Knee Extension *(post-surgery only)*

**Description:** Stand with a resistance band around the back of the operative knee, attached to a stable anchor. Begin with knee slightly bent, heel and foot on the floor, trunk upright. Straighten the knee using the quad muscle. Hold 6 seconds. Return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 2 sets of 12 reps, up to 3 times per day.  
**Purpose:** Improves quad strength and walking.

---

#### 🟢 Single-Leg Heel Raises *(post-surgery only)*

**Description:** Stand with hands on stable surface, feet shoulder-width apart. Rise onto both toes over 2 seconds, then bend the non-operative knee to stand only on the operative foot. Lower slowly over 4 seconds.  
**Dosage:** 2–3 sets of 12 reps, 1–3 times per day.  
**Prerequisite:** Master double-leg heel raises first.

---

#### 🟢 Patellar Mobilization — Medial and Lateral *(post-surgery only)*

**Description:** Ensure operative knee is straight and fully relaxed. Use thumb and index finger to find the edges of the kneecap. Move the kneecap firmly side to side 20 times. Avoid irritating the skin or incisions.  
**Purpose:** Helps bend and straighten the knee more easily; reduces knee tightness.

---

## Phase 2 (approx. 2–3 weeks post-surgery)

**Entry criteria:** Knee extension within 5° of full; flexion ≥ 110°; 20 straight leg raises without difficulty.  
**Goals:** Full range of motion, increasing strength, resume daily activities (walking, stairs, getting in/out of car).

Phase 2 transitions from floor-based Phase 1 exercises to resistance-band functional exercises.

---

### Strength (continued from Phase 1)

Same: Seated Passive-Assisted Knee Extensions, Quadriceps Set, Standing Hip Extensions, Side-Lying Hip Abduction, Standing Terminal Knee Extension.

---

### Functional Exercises — New in Phase 2

---

#### 🟢 Straight Leg Raises With Resistance

**Description:** Same as Phase 1 SLR but with added resistance (weight or band). Lie on back, non-operative leg bent to 90°. Tighten the operative quad fully, lift to the height of the other knee using a slow and controlled motion. Return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps, 3 times per day.

---

#### 🟢 Standing Hip Flexion With Resistance

**Description:** Loop a resistance band around a secure object. Place one ankle inside the loop and step back until there is slight tension. Stand tall, tighten the quad to straighten/lock the knee. Lift the banded leg slightly and kick forward slowly. Return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps each leg, 1–3 times per day.  
**Prerequisite:** Progresses from Straight Leg Raises.

---

#### 🟢 Standing Single-Leg Hip Extension With Resistance

**Description:** Band looped around a secure object, one ankle inside, step back for tension. Stand tall, tighten the quad to lock the knee. Lift the leg in the band slightly and kick it backward slowly. Return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps each leg, 1–3 times per day.  
**Prerequisite:** Progresses from Prone Straight Leg Raises.

---

#### 🟢 Standing Single-Leg Hip Abduction With Resistance

**Description:** Band looped to a secure anchor at ankle height, step sideways for slight tension. Stand tall. Lift the banded leg slightly and move it out to the side, keeping the knee straight. Avoid leaning the trunk. Return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps each leg, 1–3 times per day.  
**Prerequisite:** Progresses from Side-Lying Hip Abduction.

#### 🟢 Standing Anterior Reach

**Description:** Stand straight, hands at lower abdomen. Bend one knee as you reach the opposite foot forward as far as possible under control. Lightly touch the heel down for stability. Return to standing. Moving slowly increases the benefit.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps each leg, 1–3 times per day.  
**Purpose:** Improves stability and strength of the stationary leg.

---

#### 🟢 Standing Hip Hinge

**Description:** Balance on one leg with knee slightly bent. Keep the other leg straight. Hinge forward at the hips, torso toward the ground until a slight hamstring stretch is felt, while the straight leg lifts behind you. Keep the low back neutral — do not round forward. Return slowly.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps each leg, 1–3 times per day.  
**Note:** Can add light weight as strength improves (with PT guidance).

---

#### 🟢 Side Step Up

**Description:** Stand beside a 2–6 inch step or platform. Place the nearest foot on the step. Lift yourself up through the heel while simultaneously lifting the opposite knee to 90°. Return to start slowly, stepping completely off the step.  
**Purpose:** Develops lateral strength and stability.

## Phase 3 (approx. 7–10 weeks post-surgery)

**Entry criteria:** Full ROM equal to non-injured leg; normal daily activities without difficulty; double-leg squat to ≥ 60° with proper mechanics; IKDC self-assessment score ≥ 7.  
**Goals:** Maintain full ROM; quad strength to 70–75% of non-surgical leg; single-leg balance; basic hopping in place; begin sports-specific activities.

Phase 3 continues most Phase 2 exercises with increased resistance, and adds Front Step Ups plus plyometric jump training.

---

### Exercises (Phase 3)

The following Phase 2 exercises continue with added resistance: Seated Passive Knee Extensions, Standing Hip Flexion with Resistance, Standing Single-Leg Hip Extension with Resistance, Standing Single-Leg Hip Abduction with Resistance, Double-Leg Quarter Squats, Double-Leg Heel Raises, Single-Leg Heel Raises, Standing Anterior Reach, Standing Hip Hinge, Side Step Up.

---

#### 🟢 Front Step Ups *(new in Phase 3)*

**Description:** Stand behind a 6-inch step. Place one foot on the step and step up, weight through the heel. As you step up, lift the other knee forward until the thigh reaches 90° at the hip. Return slowly, stepping completely off the step. Switch legs.  
**Dosage:** 12 reps each leg, 1–3 times per day.

---

#### Plyometrics / Jump Training *(new in Phase 3)*

Phase 3 introduces basic plyometrics, supervised and progressed by your physical therapist or athletic trainer:

- **Double-leg hopping in place** with proper mechanics and no pain
- **Landing focus:** knees vertically aligned with toes, equal weight distribution, no inward knee bend (valgus collapse)
- Progress to single-leg hopping only when double-leg form is solid

Specific volume, intensity and progression are individually prescribed by your rehabilitation team.

---

## Phases 4–5 (Return-to-Sport Progression)

**Entry criteria (Phase 4):** Completion of Phase 3 goals and clearance by PT/athletic trainer.  
**Note:** Exercises are individualized from here — no universal exercise list is prescribed. Programs are tailored to your specific sport, work or activity.

---

### Phase 4

**Focus:** Retraining running form and technique before any cutting or change of direction.

| Activity | Progression |
|----------|-------------|
| Walking | Start on treadmill |
| Jogging | Increase speed and duration |
| Running | Increase speed and distance |
| Running off treadmill | Straight-line jog-runs on even surfaces (e.g., track) |
| Plyometrics | Double-leg jump and land → progress to single-leg |

**Landing form cues:**
- Knees aligned vertically over toes (viewed from front)
- Equal weight distribution on both legs
- Avoid inward knee bend (valgus) — especially important for naturally knock-kneed individuals
- Progress: add forward and side-to-side movements → repetitive jumps once form is consistent

---

### Phase 5

**Focus:** Higher-level cutting, deceleration, and reactive movements.

| Activity Type | Examples |
|---------------|---------|
| Direction change | 45-degree cuts, wide S-curves, figure-eight running |
| Deceleration | Start-stop exercises |
| Plyometrics | Single-leg hopping with functional movements |
| Reactive drills | Unplanned movement patterns |
| Sport-specific | Tailored to your sport or work activity |

Strengthening exercises continue throughout Phases 4–5 with the goal of increasing functional strength for running and jumping activities.

---

## Return to Sport

**Clearance is based on (no single validated test — typically includes):**
- Battery of physical tests: strength, power, hop tests, balance
- IKDC subjective knee score and ACL-RSI (psychological readiness) assessment
- Clearance by surgeon and PT/athletic trainer
- Successful completion of sport-specific individual and team practice activities
- Ability to run, cut, and pivot without hesitation or compensation

**Key guidelines:**
- Return to practice first, then return to full play
- ~9 months post-surgery is a common benchmark — but readiness, not time, is the deciding factor
- Follow a structured injury-prevention program specific to your sport (e.g., FIFA 11+, STOP)
- Train proper landing and deceleration technique to reduce retear risk
- Both the reconstructed and the uninjured leg carry similar retear risk — train both
